body: >-

About

This seminar organized by the Transition Design Lab explores the relationship between artificial intelligence and decision-making within organizations. Designed as an experimental and reflective space, it questions the role of AI not only as a tool but as a potential actor in organizational decision processes .
Based on concrete situations shared by partner organizations – SNCF, MAIF, BPCE, FNCuma, 110 bis (Ministry of Education), Artemis – participants will be invited to:


describe real decision-making situations , with or without AI,


identify the parameters, actors, and values involved ,


imagine how AI could be introduced into these situations and assess its impact on coordination, hierarchy, and responsibility.


The seminar will result in a collective mapping of AI uses and potentials in organizations, as well as a shared reflection on how AI transforms decision cultures and practices.

Program

09:30 — Welcome and introduction
Opening remarks by Giulia Marcocchia and Annie Gentès .

10.00 — Guest talk: AI and organizational decision-making
Presentation by Chantal Joie La Marle , Head of CSR at SNCF, on AI-assisted decision models. 

11.00 — Workshop 1: Identifying decision situations
Collective work: identify and describe concrete decision-making situations in participating organizations, specifying the actors, tools and criteria involved.

14.00 — Workshop 2: Introducing AI into decision-making
Small group work imagining how AI could be embedded into the previously identified situations - exploring roles, boundaries, and consequences.

16.00 — Collective discussion
Group presentations followed by a discussion on organizational, ethical, and human implications.

17h00 — Wrap-up and perspectives
Summary of key insights and presentation of future research directions.

Outputs

A collective mapping of current and potential uses of AI in decision-making.


A written restitution published on the Transition Design Lab website.

Call to participation
Join the seminar
The seminar is open to partners of the Design of Organizations Chair and by invitation from the Transition Design Lab . Free participation, limited seats.
